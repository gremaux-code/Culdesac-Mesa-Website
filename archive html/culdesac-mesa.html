About Culdesac

We build community.

Culdesac is a real estate developer and neighborhood manager that builds walkable, mixed-use communities designed around belonging, transportation choice, and thriving local businesses.

We built Culdesac Tempe — the first car-free neighborhood built from scratch in the U.S. — which is now home to over 350 residents and 23 local businesses directly on the light rail.

Unlike conventional developers, Culdesac stays. We don't just build neighborhoods — we manage them, program them, and grow them over time alongside the people who live there.

What's Planned

A neighborhood that grows gently from south to north

Culdesac Mesa uses a transect approach — lower-scale homes along the southern edge, transitioning to a more vibrant mixed-use core as you move north toward University Drive. This creates a natural bridge between the historic neighborhoods to the south and the energy of Downtown Mesa.

Three Neighborhood Zones

Up to 3 stories

Neighborhood

Compact, walkable blocks composed of small-footprint buildings — primarily townhomes — that reflect the scale and rhythm of the adjacent historic neighborhoods. Phase 1 begins here with approximately 140 for-sale townhomes.

3–12 stories

Village

The social and commercial heart. Housing, shops, restaurants, and community spaces organized around a connected network of streets and public spaces. This is where the daily life of the neighborhood comes together.

Mid- to high-rise

Campus Core

A higher-intensity district along University Drive that could support the long-term expansion of ASU's Mesa Campus, student housing, and complementary mixed-use development.

The Story of Site 17

Two decades waiting.
A city ready for what comes next.

This 26-acre site in the heart of Downtown Mesa — known as Site 17 — has remained largely vacant for over twenty years, despite multiple redevelopment efforts. It sits at the intersection of University Drive and Mesa Drive, surrounded by a city that has been steadily investing in its downtown through arts, culture, light rail, and the expansion of Arizona State University.

In 2019, Mesa City Council adopted the Transform 17 plan — a community-driven framework that envisioned this site as a walkable, mixed-use neighborhood connecting to Downtown, light rail, and surrounding destinations.

In 2023, Mesa selected Culdesac to develop the site. A Development Agreement was adopted by City Council in October 2025. Now, it's time to build.
